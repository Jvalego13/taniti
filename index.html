Discover pristine beaches, lush rainforests, and unforgettable adventures in this hidden Pacific paradise.

Quick Facts

  • Power outlets are 120V
  • Alcohol is not served from 12am - 9am
  • Legal drinking age is 18
  • English is widely spoken in town but limited in rural areas
  • There is one hospital and several clinics
  • Low violent crime, watch for pickpocketing
  • U.S. dollar is the primary currency; Euros, Yen, and major credit cards are accepted
  • Many national holidays may affect business hours

Activities

Taniti may be small but there is much to do! From beautiful beaches to fiery volcanoes. If you are into history, visit the local museum. Looking for something high speed? Zip over to Taniti Lines and go ziplining through the rainforest.

Lodging

Taniti offers a wide variety of lodging options, ranging from an inexpensive hostel to a large, four-star resort. There are many small, family-owned hotels, as well as a growing number of bed and breakfasts.

Dining

Taniti currently operates 10 restaurants, with five serving primarily local fish and rice, three offering American-style meals, and two specializing in Pan-Asian cuisine. If you would prefer to stay in, there are several grocery stores as well.

Transportation

Taniti offers a wide variety of transportation options. City busses run from 5am to 11pm. Taxis service Taniti City, and there are rental cars available as well.

Want to be more environmentally friendly?
Taniti also has bikes available to rent (helmets are required by law). Taniti is fairly flat and most attractions are within walking distance.
